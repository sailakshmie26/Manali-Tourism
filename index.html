    Beas River valley, Manali

    Manali, nestled in the picturesque Beas River valley, is a gift of the Himalayas to the world. Legend has it that Manali derives its name from the Hindu sage Manu, who is believed to be the progenitor of the human race. The town is named “Manu-Alaya,” which translates to “the abode of Manu.” A rustic enclave that once lay dormant, Manali now stands as one of India’s most popular destinations. Its transformation from a sleepy village to a bustling town is a testament to its rich cultural heritage and age-old traditions.
    Manali’s allure lies in its cool climate and snow-capped mountains. Tourists flock here to escape the scorching heat of the plains. Imagine waking up to a glorious sunrise, surrounded by breathtaking views—the glacial waters of the Beas River meandering through the valley, inviting adventure enthusiasts for rowing, white water rafting, and river crossing. So, whether you’re a seeker of legends, an adventurer, or simply captivated by the Himalayas, Manali awaits with open arms!

    Languages: Hindi, Punjabi & English are understood and spoken by the people engaged in tourism trade. The local people usually speaks in the kulluvi dialect.

    Clothing essentials: With increasing altitude, temperature falls in the valley and weather can change very abruptly in this region. Thunderstorms and snowfalls do cause a sharp drop in temperature turning an otherwise warm day into a cold one in a matter of minutes.
    Woolens are essential when visiting Manali. In summers, light woolens with a scarf for the evenings will have you prepared should it get chilly. Remember, Manali’s weather can be unpredictable, so layering is key. Also remember to add a basic first aid kit and AMS medications if venturing to high-altitude areas.
    Pack wisely and enjoy your Himalayan adventure!

    They say the antidote to exhaustion is not rest but tranquil nature. Manali offers a variety of thrilling adventure activities for adrenaline junkies.Here are some exciting options:

    Paragliding: Soar through the skies and enjoy breathtaking views of the Himalayas. Solang Valley is a popular spot for paragliding in Manali.

    River Rafting: The Beas River provides an exhilarating rafting experience. The rapids range from easy to challenging, making it suitable for both beginners and experienced rafters.

    Zorbing: Roll down gentle slopes inside a transparent inflatable ball at Solang Valley. It’s a fun and unique activity that offers stunning views.

    Skiing: During the winter season, Manali becomes a skiing paradise. Head to Solang Valley or the nearby Rohtang Pass for some thrilling downhill skiing.

    Trekking: Explore the scenic trails around Manali. Popular treks include the Hampta Pass, Bhrigu Lake, and Chandrakhani Pass. Each trek offers stunning landscapes and a chance to connect with nature.

    Mountain Biking: Rent a mountain bike and explore the rugged terrain. Ride through apple orchards, pine forests, and quaint villages.

    Camping: Spend a night under the starry sky. Many campsites near Manali offer bonfires, stargazing, and a peaceful escape from city life.

    Remember to check the weather conditions and safety guidelines before participating in any adventure activity. Enjoy your thrilling experiences in Manali!


    Famous Destinations In Manali

    Manali, nestled in the picturesque Himachal Pradesh, offers a plethora of tourist attractions. Here are some must-visit places in Manali:

    Hadimba Devi Temple: Located amidst the snow-covered hills, this unique shrine is dedicated to Hidimba Devi. The temple’s architecture and the three-day Hidimba Devi Festival make it a top attraction. Entry Fee: There is no entry fee to visit the Hadimba Devi Temple. Timings: It welcomes its devotees from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM on all days and the recommended exploration time is 1-2 hours.

    Solang Valley: Known for adventure sports, Solang Valley offers paragliding, zorbing, horse riding, and stunning views. During winters, it transforms into a snowy wonderland, perfect for skiing. Entry Fee: You don’t need to pay anything to anyone except for the price of the adrenaline-fuelled activities you wish to try. Timings: The Solang Valley is an all-season destination and hence, can be explored at any time of the year.

    Manu Temple: This Pagoda-style temple is dedicated to the sage Manu, believed to be the creator of the human race. It offers glimpses into history, spirituality, and fascinating architecture. Entry Fee: To visit the Manu Temple, you don’t have to pay anything to anyone. Timings: The opening and closing timings of this religious shrine are from 5:00 AM in the morning to 9:00 PM at night and it welcomes people on all days.

    Rohtang Pass: This scenic stretch connects Manali to Spiti and Lahaul. Popular for adventure sports like sledge rides, mountain biking, and skiing, it has been featured in several Bollywood movies. Entry Fee: As such, there is no entry fee, but you need to obtain a special permit from the government authorities of Himachal Pradesh state to travel to this location. Timings: Depending on the weather conditions, the Rohtang Pass opens its door for its visitors either from May to October or June to October. Hence, it’s the only time when you can visit it and explore its surroundings.

    Vashisht Temple: Known for its hot springs, Vashisht Temple offers a spiritual experience. The nearby Vashisht village is also worth exploring. Entry Fee: There is no entry fee to visit this temple in Manali. Timings: It opens its door for its devotees from 7:00 AM to 1:00 PM and then from 2:00 PM to 9:00 PM on all days.

    Jogini Waterfalls: Jogini Waterfalls is a popular sightseeing place in Manali where you can enjoy a trek to witness the natural phenomena of the cascading waterfall falling from a height of 160 feet. The trekking route is so scenic that it mesmerizes almost all the trekkers as it passes through orchards and pine trees, giving you a glimpse of the dazzling river Beas and the peaks of Rohtang Pass.

    Bhrigu Lake: Bhrigu Lake, also referred to as the Pool of Gods, surrounded by evergreen tree forests and alpine meadows, is a majestic lake that draws the attention of tourists, especially trekkers every year who are wanting to feel the high adrenaline rush in their bodies. Perched at an altitude of approximately 4000 feet above sea level, this water body offers a challenging yet worth trying trekking trail that gives you a kind of experience you will not forget in your life. Entry Fee: Entry to Bhrigu Lake is free for all its visitors. Timings: As a localite or a tourist, you can pay a visit to this lake at any time as it remains open for 24 hours.

    The Great Himalayan National Park: The most sought-after tourist destination in Himachal Pradesh state, the Great Himalayan National Park is a perfect spot for people who love being around animals all the time. It’s a natural habitat that provides shelter to over 31 species of mammals, 181 species of birds, and 375 species of fauna along with 1000 different species of plants. Entry Fee: To visit the Great Himalayan National Park, you’ll need to pay INR 50 per person for Indians and INR 200 per person for foreigners. For Indian students, it charges INR 30 per head and for foreign students, the cost is INR 100 per head. If you have a still camera or a video camera with you, you’ll need to pay an extra charge. Timings: It opens its door for its visitors from 10:00 AM to 5:30 PM on all days of the week and the ideal exploration time is 3-4 hours.

    Some of the other destinations includes Rahala Falls, Museum of Himachal Culture and Folk Art, Arjun Gufa, The Manali Gompa etc. Remember to explore the local culture, try Himachali cuisine, and enjoy the breathtaking views while you’re in Manali!

    How To Reach

    Air: The airport at Bhuntar is 50km from Manali from where taxis and buses are readily available. You can contact us for taxi services.

    Train: Broad gauge trains travel up to Pathankot, from where a narrow gauge train takes you to Joginder Nagar. The distance between Joginder Nagar and Manali has to be covered by road transport.

    Road: Manali is well connected by road with Delhi, Chandigarh, Dehradun, Haridwar, Shimla, Dharamshala and Chamba. Deluxe, semi-deluxe, AC, Volvo buses ply regularly to these places.

    Car Parkings: Tourists can park their cars at the parking lot near Manali main market. It is small walk from the Mall Road.
